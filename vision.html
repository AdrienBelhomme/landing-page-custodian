Built in minutes · No brief required

Too many Shopify pages still ship without real decision support. They carry paid traffic, campaign spend, and revenue risk — yet they are often built from templates, habits, or intuition. Teams have more tools and more data than ever. But page decisions are still fragile. This gap should not exist anymore.

Thesis

We believe high-impact pages should not ship without contextual intelligence behind them. A page is not just a layout. It is a decision interface. And decision interfaces require decision engines.

Core claim

We’re building Custodian so it becomes impossible to ship a Shopify page without contextual intelligence — like shipping modern software without tests and safeguards.

Standard we’re building toward

Just as modern software is expected to ship with tests and safeguards, high-stakes pages should ship with decision guidance and signal awareness. Not as an extra step. As a built-in layer. This is the standard we are building toward.

What becomes unacceptable

Our goal is to make it increasingly hard to ship a low-quality page when real context and signals are available. Not by blocking teams — but by giving them clarity before they publish.

What Custodian is

Custodian exists as a decision layer for Shopify pages. It reads store context. It interprets behavior signals. It recommends the next best move. It remembers outcomes. So teams can move faster — with less cognitive load and less blind risk.

Human role

The operator stays in control. The system carries the analysis. Judgment is supported — not replaced.

We’re building Custodian so that shipping a page without contextual decision intelligence becomes the exception — not the norm.
